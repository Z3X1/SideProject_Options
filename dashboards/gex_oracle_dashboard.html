Market Indicators
DVOL (Deribit Volatility Index)
Deribit's 30-day implied volatility index for BTC. Reflects the market's priced expectation for future price swings. Higher DVOL = greater uncertainty.
FR (Funding Rate)
Periodic payment between longs and shorts in perpetual futures. FR > 0%: longs pay shorts (overheated long side). FR < 0%: shorts pay longs (short-dominated). Resets every 8 hours.
L/S (Long/Short Ratio)
Number of accounts holding long positions divided by those holding short positions. L/S > 2 indicates extreme short overweight — used as a contrarian signal.
OI (Open Interest)
Total number of outstanding, unsettled contracts in the market. Rising OI combined with a FR directional shift produces a strong directional signal.
Options Terminology
GEX (Gamma Exposure)
Aggregate gamma position held by market makers. Positive GEX = MMs act as stabilizers (counter-hedging). Negative GEX = MMs act as amplifiers (directional hedging).
Gamma Flip
The critical price at which GEX transitions from positive to negative. Spot above the flip (POS Regime) = relatively stable. Spot below (NEG Regime) = volatility self-reinforcing.
PCR (Put/Call Ratio)
Put OI divided by Call OI. PCR > 1: put-heavy (defensive stance). PCR < 1: call-heavy (offensive stance). Must be analyzed alongside cross-expiry structure.
Call Wall / Put Wall
Strikes with heavy OI concentration. Call Wall creates overhead resistance. Put Wall provides downside support — strongest when price is ATM (at-the-money).
GEX Framework Terms
Gamma-MP (Gamma Magnet Point)
The price with the highest gamma concentration. Acts as a gravitational attractor for spot price. Market maker delta-hedging flows cause spot to gravitate toward this level. Used as the primary anchor for the settlement median forecast.
POS Regime / NEG Regime
POS (Positive Gamma Regime): Spot > Gamma Flip. MMs act as stabilizers; volatility self-suppresses. NEG (Negative Gamma Regime): Spot < Gamma Flip. MMs act as amplifiers; volatility self-reinforces.
SS (Shock Scenario)
The deep downside scenario in the framework. Sufficient conditions: sustained deep negative FR + external catalyst + break below Gamma Flip + sufficient time horizon. P(SS) is calculated using the product method (Rule #8).
Layer 1 / Layer 2
Layer 1: resistance / support derived directly from GEX structure. Layer 2: broad resistance zone from technical indicators (EMA, MACD, etc.). In POS Regime, the two layers are merged. In NEG Regime, they are strictly separated.
Technical Indicators
MACD (DIF / DEA / Histogram)
DIF = short EMA minus long EMA. DEA = signal line of DIF. DIF < DEA = death cross (bearish). DIF > DEA = golden cross (bullish). The framework requires signals to meet minimum lifetime thresholds before they are considered valid.
RSI (Relative Strength Index)
Momentum oscillator ranging 0–100. RSI < 30 = oversold. RSI > 70 = overbought. The framework distinguishes between a mechanical rollover (effect ×0.5) and genuine oversold (effect ×1.0).
EMA (Exponential Moving Average)
Weighted moving average giving more weight to recent prices. The framework tracks EMA5 / 10 / 30 / 200. The zone between EMA200(4h) and EMA30(2h) forms the Layer 2 broad resistance zone.
